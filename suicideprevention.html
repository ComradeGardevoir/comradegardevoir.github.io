WARNING SIGNS

Some risk factors that lead to suicidal thoughts or actions are drug misuse, social isolation, depression, mental disorder, and childhood abuse. Warning signs of suicide are giving away things, talking about suicide or death in general, more drug use, sleeping pattern changes, and mood swings. If you know someone who has had suicidal thoughts or actions, or know of someone showing warning signs, please contact a trusted adult or the suicide helpline.

THINK ABOUT IT

If you are thinking about suicide, contact the National Suicide Prevention Lifeline at 1-800-273-8255. Get help immediately. Many problems can be resolved if you take a moment and talk with others about the situation. This hotline is completely confidential, and nobody except for you will ever know anything about the call.

Remember, there is always someone who loves you and cares about you.

Talking with loved ones is also sometimes very helpful to those with suicidal thoughts or actions. Please do not hesitate to call the above hotline if you are thinking about suicide or know someone who is.
